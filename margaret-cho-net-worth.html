Margaret Cho Overview and Wiki

Early life

Margaret Cho‘s career in the entertainment industry is a remarkable story of resilience, versatility, and groundbreaking achievements. Born on December 5, 1968, in San Francisco, Cho’s journey into comedy began in the clubs adjacent to her parents’ bookstore.

Her early experiences in these clubs laid the foundation for a career that would see her become one of the most influential figures in stand-up comedy.

Early Stand-up and All-American Girl

Cho’s career in stand-up comedy took off after several years of honing her material in clubs. Her unique voice in comedy, which often critiqued social and political issues, especially regarding race and sexuality, quickly gained recognition.

In 1994, Cho’s talent led to the creation of the ABC sitcom All-American Girl, which was based on her stand-up routine. This show was significant as it was one of the first to feature an East Asian family prominently.

However, the show faced challenges, including criticism for stereotyping and network pressure on Cho regarding her appearance, leading to its cancellation after one season.

Overcoming Challenges and Diversifying Her Career

Following the cancellation of All-American Girl, Cho faced personal struggles, including addiction. However, she overcame these challenges and returned to stand-up with renewed vigor.

In 1999, she wrote and performed in her one-woman show, I’m the One That I Want, which won critical acclaim and was named Performance of the Year by New York magazine. This show was a turning point, allowing Cho to discuss her experiences in show business, her struggles with ethnicity and body image, and her triumph over addiction.

Acting Roles and Emmy Nomination

As an actress, Cho has demonstrated remarkable versatility. She played roles in films like It’s My Party and Face/Off, where she acted alongside John Travolta.

On television, she was part of the cast of Drop Dead Diva on Lifetime Television, playing the role of Teri Lee, a paralegal assistant. Her outstanding performance as Kim Jong-il on 30 Rock earned her a nomination for the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Guest Actress in a Comedy Series in 2012.

Advocacy, Fashion, and Music

Apart from her work in comedy and acting, Cho has been a vocal advocate for LGBT rights and has engaged in various humanitarian efforts.

She has also ventured into fashion, founding a clothing line called High Class Cho, and into music, releasing albums that blend humor with societal commentary. Her involvement in these diverse fields showcases her multifaceted talent and her ability to influence a wide range of audiences.

Recent Projects and Continued Influence

In recent years, Cho has continued to make significant contributions to entertainment and advocacy. She co-starred in the 2022 film Fire Island, a portrayal of the LGBT Asian American experience.

Cho also hosts a podcast called The Margaret Cho, featuring guests primarily from show business, and continues to be a prominent figure in stand-up comedy, television, and film.

Margaret Cho’s career is a testament to her talent, resilience, and ability to break barriers. From her early days in stand-up comedy to her current status as a multifaceted entertainer and advocate, Cho’s journey is an inspiring story of overcoming adversity and making a lasting impact in the entertainment industry.

Personal life

Cho married artist Al Ridenour in 2003. Al Ridenour was involved with The Cacophony Society and the Art of Bleeding, known for their performance art and unconventional events. Their marriage was often described by Cho as unconventional and non-traditional.

In interviews and her comedy, Cho has spoken about the open nature of their relationship. However, the couple separated in 2014, and Cho confirmed their separation in December of that year. She referred to herself as divorced in an April 2015 profile.

As for children, Margaret Cho does not have any. She has not publicly spoken about having children or expressed a desire to have them in her interviews or public statements.

Cho’s career and advocacy work have been her primary focus, as evident from her extensive work in comedy, acting, and support for various social causes, particularly LGBTQ rights.

FAQs about Margaret Cho

Who is Margaret Cho?

She is a multi-talented American comedian, actor, musician, advocate, entrepreneur, and artist known for her stand-up routines, acting roles, and advocacy work.

What is Margaret Cho’s background?

Born in San Francisco in 1968 to a Korean family, Cho grew up in a diverse neighborhood which influenced her worldview. She faced bullying and personal challenges, including sexual abuse, in her youth.

How did she start her career in comedy?

She began her stand-up career at age 14, winning a contest to open for Jerry Seinfeld in the early ’90s. She then moved to Los Angeles and became a popular act on the college circuit.

What was Margaret Cho’s breakthrough in television?

Cho’s breakthrough was the ABC sitcom All-American Girl in 1994, which was groundbreaking but controversial and led to personal and professional challenges for Cho.

Has she written any one-woman shows?

Yes, Cho’s acclaimed one-woman show I’m The One That I Want in 1999 received great reviews, and she controlled its distribution and sales, which was unusual at the time.

What other significant tours has she done?

She has had several successful tours, including Notorious C.H.O. in 2001, Revolution in 2003, and Assassin in 2005.

Has she been involved in any music or fashion projects?

Yes, she has endeavors in fashion, with her clothing line, and in music, including her comedy music album Cho Dependent.

What are some of Margaret Cho’s advocacy works?

She is a vocal supporter of LGBT rights and has won awards for her humanitarian efforts on behalf of women, Asian Americans, and the LGBT community.

Has she faced any personal struggles?

She has a long family history with depression and has openly discussed her struggles, including a period of substance abuse and suicide attempts. She credits her sense of humor with helping her through dark times.

What about Margaret Cho’s personal life and relationships?

She has been linked to several notable figures, including director Quentin Tarantino and artist Al Ridenour, with whom she had an open marriage.

Is Margaret Cho bisexual?

Yes, She has identified as truly bisexual, discussing the complexities and misunderstandings associated with bisexuality.

Has she been involved in any controversies?

She faced backlash for her portrayal of a North Korean General at the 2015 Golden Globe Awards, which some viewed as racist. She has defended her actions, citing her North Korean heritage.

What was Margaret Cho’s relationship with Joan Rivers?

She greatly admired Joan Rivers and saw her as a mentor. They co-hosted E!’s Fashion Police together.
