Early Life & Biography

Christina Rene Hendricks was born on May 3, 1975, in Knoxville, Tennessee, the U.S. to her parents Jackie Sue Hendricks and Robert Hendricks. Her father was a Forest Service employee, and her mother was a psychologist.

Her family frequently relocated due to her father’s job, first in Georgia, and then to Portland, Oregon, from where she completed her elementary schooling. At the age of nine, Hendricks moved to Twin Falls, Idaho, where she joined a local theater group. Later, her family finally settled in Fairfax, Virginia, and she attended Fairfax High School.

Personal Life

Hendricks got married to Geoffrey Arend in 2009. After being together for ten years, the couple divorced in 2019. She does not have any children and has been able to keep her personal life away from the limelight and is completely focused on her career.

Career

Christina Hendricks started her career as a receptionist and shampoo girl at a salon before appearing on the cover of Seventeen magazine. At the age of 18, she moved to New York City to study acting in Virginia Commonwealth University’s drama school.

Hendricks appeared in several commercials and also worked as a professional model between the ages of 18 and 27. In her early twenties, she pursued a career working in the administrative sector of the music business. At the same time, she appeared in the commercials of Carl’s Jr. and Dr. Pepper. In 1997, she starred in the music video for Everclear’s “One Hit Wonder.”

Hendricks made her television debut in the anthology series Undressed of MTV. She starred in the Showtime comedy series, Beggars and Choosers, as an intern. In 2000, she did a guest role on the series Angel. Subsequently, Hendricks appeared in popular movies like The Big Time (2002), ER (2002), The Court (2003), and Firefly (2002–2003).

Hendricks portrayed the role of Joan Holloway in the AMC series Mad Men which received much appreciation from critics as well as fans. For the role, she was nominated in six Primetime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series over the course of its seven seasons.

In 2007, Hendricks made her feature film debut in La Cucina. In the same year, she starred in the thriller film South of Pico, and NBC series Life. She was also seen in the music video for The Ghost Inside on “Broken Bells.” Later, she did a recurring role in the action-thriller Drive, which was directed by Nicolas Winding Refn. The following year, she played a supporting role in the drama film Ginger & Rosa.

Hendricks also lent her voice for the character Sam Harper in the 2011 racing video game Need for Speed: The Run. In 2014, she played the lead role in the fantasy movie, Lost River which received mixed reviews. She played the character of a modeling agent in the 2016 thriller film The Neon Demon.

In 2018, Hendricks appeared in the Amazon Prime series The Romanoffs. She was also the cast of the comedy-crime series Good Girls.

Awards & Achievements

Christina Hendricks has won several awards in her illustrious career. She also won two Screen Actors Guild Awards for Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Drama Series, as well as an additional three nominations.

Hendricks won the Critics’ Choice Television Awards in the category of Best Supporting Actress in a Drama Series in 2011 and 2012. She also won the Gold Derby Awards for Supporting Actress – Drama. In 2009, she won the Golden Nymph Awards for her work in the movie, Mad Men. In 2006, she was given the SyFy Genre Awards for Best Special Guest/Television.

Hendricks has been nominated for Primetime Emmy Awards six times, Gold Derby Awards seven times, Behind the Voice Actors Awards five times.
